Bruce Hobbs Scholarship


The Scholarship was established with a generous donation from Dr. Bruce Hobbs. Bruce Hobbs (born 23 October 1936) is an Australian structural geologist and science administrator. He is a Research Fellow of the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation and Adjunct Professor, University of Western Australia. He was elected a Fellow of the Australian Academy of Science in 1991. He was appointed the first Chief Scientist in Western Australia in 2003. Hobbs has popularly defined structural geology as the study of "the way in which rocks get buried and deformed and metamorphosed."

The purpose of this scholarship is to support continuing undergraduate students who are undertaking Geology as a major and demonstrate excellence in their academic work. It requires a minimum of 6 GPA.

Value: AUD 6000 to be paid as a lump sum after 31 March, 2020 (Census Date)

Duration: 1 Year

Criteria: Undergraduate Students with Geology major, with minimum of 6 GPA

Application Closure Date: 3, Dec, 2019

John Eccles Scholarship


The Scholarship was established with a generous donation from the family of Sir John Eccles. John Eccles (27 January 1903 – 2 May 1997) was an Australian neurophysiologist and philosopher who won the 1963 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his work on the synapse. He shared the prize with Andrew Huxley and Alan Lloyd Hodgkin.

The purpose of this scholarship is to support continuing undergraduate students who are undertaking Biological Studies as a major and demonstrate excellence in their academic work.

Value: AUD 5000 to be paid as a lump sum after 31 March, 2020 (Census Date)

Duration: 1 Year

Criteria: Undergraduate Students with Biological Studies major

Application Closure Date: 14 Dec, 2019

Elizabeth Blackburn Scholarship


The Scholarship was established with a generous donation from Elizabeth Blackburn. Elizabeth Blackburn, (born 26 November 1948) is an Australian-American Nobel laureate who is the former President of the Salk Institute for Biological Studies. Previously she was a biological researcher at the University of California, San Francisco, who studied the telomere, a structure at the end of chromosomes that protects the chromosome. Blackburn co-discovered telomerase, the enzyme that replenishes the telomere. For this work, she was awarded the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine, sharing it with Carol W. Greider and Jack W. Szostak, becoming the only Tasmanian-born Nobel laureate.

The purpose of this scholarship is to support female continuing undergraduate students who are undertaking Biological Studies as a major and demonstrate excellence in their academic work.

Value: AUD 6000 to be paid as a lump sum after 31 March, 2020 (Census Date)

Duration: 1 Year

Criteria: Female, Undergraduate Students with Biological Studies major

Application Closure Date: 7, Dec, 2019

Dorothy Hill Scholarship


The Scholarship was established with a generous donation from the family of Dorothy Hill. Dorothy Hill (10 September 1907 – 23 April 1997) was an Australian geologist and palaeontologist, the first female professor at an Australian university, and the first female president of the Australian Academy of Science.

The purpose of this scholarship is to support female continuing undergraduate students who are undertaking Geological Studies as a major and demonstrate excellence in their academic work.

Value: AUD 5000 to be paid as a lump sum after 31 March, 2020 (Census Date)

Duration: 1 Year

Criteria: Female, Undergraduate Students with Geological Studies major
